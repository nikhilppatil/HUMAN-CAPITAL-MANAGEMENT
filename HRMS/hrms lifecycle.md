***1. PF (Provident Fund)***



A retirement savings scheme for employees.



Both employee and employer contribute a fixed percentage (usually 12%) of the employee's basic salary every month.



The money accumulates and earns interest, helping employees save for retirement or emergencies.



Managed by the Employees’ Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO).



Employees can withdraw or transfer their PF balance under certain conditions.



**-------------------------------------------------------------------------------**



***2. ESI (Employee State Insurance)***



A health insurance and social security scheme for employees earning below a certain salary threshold.



Both employer and employee contribute to this fund.



Provides medical benefits, sickness leave, maternity leave, and other social security benefits.



Managed by the Employees’ State Insurance Corporation (ESIC).



Mostly applicable to organizations with 10 or more employees (rules may vary by region).



**-------------------------------------------------------------------------------**



***3. TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)***



A tax collection mechanism where the employer deducts income tax from the employee’s salary before paying it.



The deducted amount is deposited with the government.



TDS helps employees pay their income tax in advance throughout the year.



The final tax liability is calculated at the end of the financial year, and employees file income tax returns accordingly.



The amount of TDS depends on the employee's income and applicable tax slabs.



**-------------------------------------------------------------------------------**



***4. Loans***



Many companies offer employee loans or salary advances as a benefit.



These can be personal loans, housing loans, or advances against salary.



The loan amount is usually deducted in installments from the employee’s salary.



Loan details such as amount, tenure, and repayment schedule are tracked in the HRMS.

***UAN (for EPF in India)***



UAN (Universal Account Number) is a unique 12-digit number for EPF members in India.



It links all your EPF accounts from different employers under one number.



UAN helps you easily manage, transfer, and check your EPF balance online.



It stays the same throughout your career, regardless of job changes.



**-------------------------------------------------------------------------------**



***ESI Number (if applicable)***



ESI (Employee State Insurance) is a social security scheme for Indian employees earning below a certain salary limit.



It provides medical, sickness, maternity, disability, and dependent benefits to insured employees and their families.



Both the employer and employee contribute a fixed percentage of the employee’s wages to the ESI fund.
