**Documents Checklist When You Buy Home.**



1. Title or Sale Deed – proves the seller’s ownership.
2. Chain of Title / Mother Deed – shows how ownership passed from one person to another.
3. Encumbrance or Lien Certificate – confirms the property isn’t mortgaged or under dispute.
4. Latest Property-Tax Receipts – ensure all taxes are paid.
5. Approved Building Plan \& Zoning Clearance – confirm the structure follows local rules.
6. Completion or Occupancy Certificate – issued by the municipality when construction meets approval.
7. Mutation / Khata / Patta Extract – local-land-record entry showing the owner’s name.
8. No-Objection Certificates (NOCs) – from utilities, fire, environment, housing society, etc., if required.
9. Utility Bills – water, power, and maintenance dues cleared.
10. Possession Letter or Allotment Letter – if it’s a new or builder-sold property.
11. Agreement to Sell / Sale Agreement – terms of payment and transfer.
12. Loan Release or Discharge Certificate – if the seller had a mortgage.



**Optional but wise:**



Independent legal-title verification by a property lawyer.



Valuation and survey report to check boundaries.



RERA registration or local-authority registration for new projects.
